## Overview

Growth and resilience are not the same thing.

Most destination reporting focuses on visitor arrivals, total expenditure and year-on-year growth. Those measures describe how demand has changed. They reveal much less about what that demand depends on.

This publication introduces a portfolio diagnostic that compares the visitor expenditure mix of Australian states and territories with structurally comparable destination types. Rather than asking whether demand is growing, it asks whether the underlying portfolio is balanced, concentrated or exposed.

The accompanying interactive diagnostic allows users to explore the results by jurisdiction and visitor purpose.

## Method summary

The analysis compares visitor expenditure shares within structurally comparable destination types using expenditure-weighted benchmarks.

The destination types are:

* Urban Gateway
* Coastal Leisure
* Regional Mixed
* Remote

Differences of ±0.5 percentage points or less are treated as broadly at benchmark.

The Northern Territory is treated as a special case because the dataset does not contain a sufficiently comparable peer destination against which its portfolio concentration can be benchmarked.

The analysis uses one year of publicly available data. It is intended as a directional diagnostic to support discussion and further investigation, not as a definitive forecast or policy recommendation.

A full explanation of the analytical framework, assumptions and evidence limits is provided within the publication.
